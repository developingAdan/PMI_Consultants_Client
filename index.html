PMI/NDE TESTING SERVICES

  • Positive Material Identification (PMI) is one of the more specialized non destructive testing methods. With positive material identification the alloy composition of materials can be determined. If a material certificate is missing or it is not clear what the composition of a material is, then PMI offers the solution. Because specifications for materials used in industry are increasingly more specific, the need for PMI testing has been on an increase for the past several years. Periodic plant maintenance shutdowns are less frequent and consequently the materials used in the plant are in use longer. A wider variety of alloys that are indistinguishable to the eye are being used in process plants. When facility and inspection staff replace components, they must be able to guarantee that the new part matches required specifications. Recent industrial accidents have cost the lives of workers and heightened the awareness of the need for accurate and comprehensive PMI inspections.

Services

We specialize in:

XRF/OES PMI

XRF/OES PMI is a non-destructive testing method that utilizes X-Ray Fluorescence and Optical Emission Spectroscopy technology to accurately identify the composition of metals and alloys.

Hardness Testing

Hardness testing is a crucial component of Positive Material Identification (PMI) testing, providing critical information about the strength and durability of materials. By measuring the resistance of a material to indentation or scratching, hardness testing helps determine its suitability for specific applications and ensures compliance with industry standards.

PT/MT Testing

Penetrant Testing (PT) and Magnetic Particle Testing (MT) are two non-destructive testing methods used in Positive Material Identification (PMI) testing. PT involves applying a visible or fluorescent dye to the surface of the material, which then penetrates any surface cracks or discontinuities. MT uses magnetic fields to detect surface and subsurface discontinuities. Both methods provide critical information about the quality and safety of materials, helping to ensure compliance with industry standards and minimize the risk of failure

Ferrite Testing

Ferrite testing is a crucial component of Positive Material Identification (PMI) testing, providing valuable information about the microstructure of austenitic stainless steel materials. This test measures the proportion of ferrite in the material, which is important for determining the material's suitability for specific applications, ensuring compliance with industry standards, and minimizing the risk of corrosion or other material degradation.

Ultrasonic Testing

Ultrasonic testing is a non-destructive testing method used in Positive Material Identification (PMI) testing to evaluate the quality and integrity of materials. This method uses high-frequency sound waves to penetrate the material and detect any internal discontinuities, such as cracks, voids, or inclusions. Ultrasonic testing provides critical information about the material's suitability for specific applications and helps ensure compliance with industry standards.

RoHS Testing

RoHS testing is a critical component of Positive Material Identification (PMI) testing, ensuring compliance with the Restriction of Hazardous Substances (RoHS) Directive. This directive restricts the use of certain hazardous substances in electrical and electronic equipment, and requires that all products sold in the European Union meet strict environmental standards. RoHS testing verifies that materials and products are free of restricted substances, such as lead, cadmium, and mercury, helping to minimize the impact on the environment and human health.

Lead Testing in Paint

Lead testing in paint is an important component of Positive Material Identification (PMI) testing, ensuring compliance with regulations and minimizing potential health hazards. Lead is a toxic substance that can pose significant risks to human health, especially for children and pregnant women. Lead testing in paint verifies the absence of lead in paint used in residential and commercial buildings, helping to protect public health and the environment.

Precious Metals Testing

Precious metals testing is a critical component of Positive Material Identification (PMI) testing, ensuring the purity and authenticity of precious metals used in various applications. This testing helps to verify the presence of precious metals, such as gold, silver, and platinum, and determine their composition and quality. Precious metals testing can also verify compliance with industry standards and regulations, helping to ensure that products meet the highest quality and performance standards.

Rental Equipment

We offer rental services that allow customers to rent PMI equipment on a short-term basis, giving them the flexibility to complete projects quickly and efficiently, without having to invest in equipment. The rental equipment offering includes a range of PMI instruments and tools, including X-ray Fluorescence (XRF) spectrometers, Optical Emission Spectrometers (OES), and other advanced testing equipment. This provides customers with cost-effective and convenient access to the equipment they need, helping them to get the job done right.
